_Author's note: In what follows, "AI" largely refers to Claude/ChatGPT-style LLMs and the associated agents._

1. The primary driving force behind AI development is not automation, efficiency, nor equity, but the desire amongst the owning class to extract capital from the working class. 

2. Atrophied _perspective taking_ from over-reliance on AI is just as worrisome (if not more so) than atrophied _critical thinking_.

3. The adoption of AI hinges on exactly one proposition: not improved output or efficiency, but improved *control*. "I prefer the control I have over an agent/agents to what humans typically give me."

3. The onset of LLM-derived content has pushed humanity into a regime of plausible-sounding pseudotruth never before seen.

4. As AI automates rote coding tasks in the software sector, performative talk-past-each-other "alignment" activities will fill the gap (not new, faster feature development).
