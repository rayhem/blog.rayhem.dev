During my travels in the Netherlands I came to learn about _[stellingen](https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stelling_(proefschrift))_; a fun tradition whereby Ph.D candidates curate a list of ten _propositions_, short statements the student is able and willing to defend in front of their committee, alongside their actual dissertation. 
Prepositions Most often start out as statements about the student's doctoral work, but the ones at the end of the list tend to drift towards reflections on the culture of science, observations about life, or philosophy at large and often take a turn towards the humorous (or even self-referential).
Since I didn't graduate from the Netherlands so I never got to make my own list as part of my work, but why let that stop me?
And why stop at ten!
Here you'll find my own propositions as short, bulleted lists of thoughts about the titular subject.

Think of these as ideas I haven't had the chutzpah to turn into fully-fledged blog posts (yet) with the disclaimer that I reserve the right to _change_ my mind 😄
